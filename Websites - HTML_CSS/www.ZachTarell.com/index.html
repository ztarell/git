Welcome

I have been in Computer Science for over five years now as a Computer Programmer, but that is just the beginning of my story. I enjoy all aspects of Software Engineering but particularly Artificial Intelligence.

My Journey

I was born in Pennsylvania and I moved around a lot during my high school years. I’ve lived in PA, TX, NM and KS, going back and forth between them sometimes. I graduated high school in Kansas City then moved back to PA. My first job was as a tee-ball umpire when I was 12 and $114.82 was my first paycheck.

While my family moved again directly after high school, I decided I didn’t want to follow them at the time and began working as a hardscaper in PA. I built retaining walls, paver patios and walkways, fish ponds, etc. It was hard work but very fulfilling. After a while I decided it was time for a change and moved back down to TX, been here ever since, to be around my family again. Here, I worked at a company in Plano called Luminator, Ltd. I started at $8.25/hr and in 4 years moved to a test technician at $15.88/hr.

This was when I was lucky enough to meet my now wife and decided it was time to go back to school to get a degree. With her support, I was able to graduate from Dallas College with an A.A.S. in Software Development, with a GPA of 3.78, and then transferred to UTD and graduate with a B.S. in Software Engineering, GPA of 3.2, all within 5 years. Everywhere I’ve been, everyone I’ve met and lost along the way, and everything I’ve had to overcome has made me a stronger person and a harder worker. I am lucky and grateful for it all and can’t wait to see what’s next.

My Philosophy

Teams

I excel at team exercises and pair programming. I work great alone but better within teams. I’ve never met a challenge that wasn’t successful inside a team environment, and I’ve never met a teammate that I wasn’t able to get the most out of to accomplish our goal. No persons or people are the same, and no team is successful without cohesion.

Trust

I can change my demeanor and attitude without losing who I am and how others portray me. There are always different moods, feelings, expressions, etc. for different people, places, or events… finding those intricate settings inside of others makes them feel at ease, and should be brought to light. But, changing to those settings can damage yourself and how others might’ve thought you were. There is nothing fake about me. I let my humble upbringings and my empathetic care in others to do good drive my relationships into friendships - trust is earned. It’s the foundation of which I rely to be heard, and I put everything on myself to seed.

Adapt

I can adapt very easily to different environments and am not held back by sudden changes or unforeseen pressures. I learned this from moving around when I was younger, you need to be able to read a room and become a chameleon in order to stay calm and keep others calm as well. Pressure occurs when there is a lack of confidence around leading to bad decisions being made. Taking a step back and assessing takes way more courage than rushing through a problem.

Hobby

I also love playing music. My weapon of choice is the guitar, but like my programming skills, I’m not limited to just one. I also love sports and movies. I’m an unapologetic Philadelphia Eagles fan - but don’t worry, I’ll only remind you once a day. And I love all kinds of movies, I guess you can say I’m a movie buff. I’m a proud nerd when it comes to mathematics as well. I can go down a YouTube rabbit hole in no time. One second I’ll be looking into Bayes’ probability function and 3 hours later dumbfounded by Nash’s equilibrium. But in all seriousness, I’m a family man, Italian in origin, and I love being around them the most.
